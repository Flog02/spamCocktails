Why is Cocktail Shaking Important?

Cocktail shaking has four main purposes:

  • Chilling your liquid
  • Combining your ingredients
  • Diluting the cocktail
  • Aerating the mixture

Aeration gives cocktails a velvety, consistent texture, reduces bitterness, and enhances sweetness—making shaking an essential technique for certain drinks.

Using a Cocktail Shaker

This technique is used for cocktails with spirits, juices, creams, fruits, or syrups that need mixing, chilling, and aeration.

How Long Does it Take to Shake a Cocktail?

Shake for a slow count of ten seconds, or until ice forms on the outside of the shaker. Avoid over-shaking with regular bar ice. Use larger cubes to feel the right point for straining.

Top Tip: Most drinks need less than 15 seconds of shaking!

How to Use a Cocktail Shaker

  1. Add ingredients to the shaker.
  2. Fill the shaker with ice.
  3. Secure the lid and shake vigorously over your shoulder.
  4. Shake for a slow count of ten.
  5. Strain into a chilled glass.

Advanced Shaking Techniques

The Double Shake

A time-saving technique where two cocktails are shaken at once—one in each hand. Use your dominant hand for drinks served straight up and your non-dominant hand for cocktails served on the rocks to maintain proper texture and dilution.

Top Tip: Every cocktail requires a different shake style. Once you add ice, the timer is on!

The Dry Shake and Reverse Dry Shake

A dry shake combines ingredients without ice for at least 30 seconds, essential for cocktails with egg whites, producing a smooth, frothy texture. The reverse dry shake adds ice first, then removes it before shaking again with egg white to enhance foam consistency.

Top Tip: Fine-strain to remove ice shards before the second dry shake.
